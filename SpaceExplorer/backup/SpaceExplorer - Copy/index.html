Earth

Our home planet — a pale blue dot in the vast cosmic ocean.

Scale: 12,742 km diameter

Planet Earth

The third planet from the Sun and the only known celestial body to harbor life.

Solar System

Our Sun and everything bound to it by gravity — planets, moons, asteroids, and comets.

Orion Arm

A minor spiral arm of the Milky Way Galaxy where our Solar System is located.

Milky Way Galaxy

Our home galaxy — a barred spiral with over 100 billion stars.

Local Group

A collection of more than 50 galaxies, including Andromeda and our Milky Way.

Virgo Supercluster

A mass concentration of galaxies containing the Virgo Cluster and Local Group.

Laniakea Supercluster

Our home supercluster containing over 100,000 galaxies across 520 million light-years.

Observable Universe

Everything we can possibly observe — about 93 billion light-years across.

Cosmic Web

The large-scale structure of the universe, a network of filaments made of galaxies and dark matter.

Universe

The totality of all space, time, matter, and energy, including the unobservable regions.

Multiverse

A speculative collection of potentially diverse universes beyond our own observable realm.
